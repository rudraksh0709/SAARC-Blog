BANGLADESH

Posted By: Vinay Kumar
Posted on: 14-03-26

Bangladesh, located in South Asia, is one of the world’s most densely populated countries and has a rich cultural heritage alongside a turbulent political history.

    Historical background
  • Bangladesh was part of British India until 1947, when it became East Pakistan after partition.
  • Following years of political and cultural tensions, it gained independence in 1971 after a brutal war of liberation.
  • Since independence, Bangladesh has alternated between civilian governments and military rule, with democracy gradually consolidating in the 1990s.
    Culture and society
  • Bengali is the national language, and the country’s identity is deeply tied to its literary and artistic traditions.
  • Islam is the dominant religion, but Hinduism and other faiths are also practiced.
  • The nation is known for its vibrant music, poetry, and cuisine, as well as its textile and garment industry.
  • Festivals like Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year) and Eid are widely celebrated.
    Current situation (2026)
  • Bangladesh is undergoing a major political reset. After the 2024 “Monsoon Revolution” led by Gen Z activists, long‑time Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina was ousted. Nobel laureate Muhammad Yunus led an interim government for 18 months.
  • The general election of February 2026 is seen as a chance to restore democratic legitimacy after years of contested rule. Analysts highlight the importance of policy stability, inflation control, and job creation for the next government.
  • Economically, Bangladesh faces inflationary pressures and foreign exchange challenges but remains a global leader in garment exports.
  • Socially, it continues to grapple with climate vulnerability, especially flooding and cyclones, while striving to improve education and healthcare.

In summary
Bangladesh today is a nation of resilience and cultural richness, standing at a political crossroads. Its future depends on whether democratic institutions can stabilize and whether economic reforms can balance growth with social equity and climate resilience.
