AFGHANISTAN

Posted By: Vinay Kumar
Posted on: 14-03-26

Afghanistan is a country in South-Central Asia with a long and complex history, rich culture, and difficult present circumstances.

    Historical background
  • Afghanistan has been a crossroads of civilizations for centuries, influenced by Persian, Indian, and Central Asian cultures.
  • It was part of the Silk Road, connecting trade routes between East and West.
  • Modern history has been marked by invasions, civil wars, and foreign interventions — from the Soviet occupation in the 1980s to the U.S.-led presence after 2001, and the Taliban’s return to power in 2021.
    Culture and society
  • Afghanistan is ethnically diverse, with Pashtuns, Tajiks, Hazaras, Uzbeks, and others.
  • Dari (Persian) and Pashto are the main languages.
  • Poetry, music, and traditional crafts like carpet weaving are central to Afghan culture.
  • Islam is the dominant religion, shaping social and cultural life.
    Current situation (2026)
  • The Taliban government has deepened restrictions on women and girls, banning secondary and higher education and curbing media freedom.
  • Humanitarian needs remain immense: Millions face food insecurity, displacement, and limited access to healthcare. Climate-driven droughts and natural disasters worsen the crisis.
  • Large-scale returns of Afghan refugees from Pakistan and Iran have added pressure on already fragile communities.

In summary
Afghanistan today is a nation of resilience and cultural richness, but also one of the world’s largest humanitarian crises. Its people continue to face challenges from political repression, economic fragility, and environmental stress, while holding onto traditions that reflect centuries of history.
