NEPAL

Posted By: Vinay Kumar
Posted on: 14-03-26

Nepal, a landlocked country nestled in the Himalayas, is renowned for its natural beauty, cultural diversity, and complex political journey.

    Historical background
  • Nepal’s modern identity began with King Prithvi Narayan Shah in the 18th century, who unified small principalities into a single kingdom.
  • The Rana dynasty (1846–1951) ruled with authoritarian control, followed by a constitutional monarchy.
  • A series of political upheavals — King Mahendra’s coup in 1960, the People’s Movement of 1990, and the abolition of the monarchy in 2008 — reshaped the nation.
  • The federal republican constitution of 2015 established Nepal as a secular, democratic republic.
    Culture and society
  • Nepal is ethnically diverse, with groups such as the Newar, Gurung, Magar, Tamang, and Tharu alongside Indo‑Aryan communities.
  • Nepali is the official language, but many regional languages are spoken.
  • Hinduism and Buddhism are the dominant religions, often blending in daily practices.
  • Festivals like Dashain, Tihar, and Buddha Jayanti reflect this cultural richness.
  • Nepal is globally famous for Mount Everest and trekking routes that attract adventurers from around the world.
    Current situation (2026)
  • Nepal’s politics remain unsettled, with frequent parliamentary dissolutions and disputes over constitutional interpretation. Analysts note that governance is still shaped by a historical “power‑centric mindset” inherited from monarchic and Rana traditions.
  • Economic challenges include dependence on remittances, vulnerability to climate change, and limited industrial growth.
  • Socially, Nepal is striving to expand education, healthcare, and infrastructure while balancing modernization with traditional values.

In summary
Nepal today is a nation of resilience and contrasts: deeply rooted in its Himalayan heritage and cultural diversity, yet still navigating political instability and economic fragility. Its people continue to adapt, drawing strength from history and community while facing the demands of a modern republic.
