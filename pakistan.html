PAKISTAN

Posted By: Vinay Kumar
Posted on: 14-03-26

Pakistan, officially the *Islamic Republic of Pakistan*, was founded in 1947 after the partition of British India. Its creation was rooted in the demand for a homeland for Muslims in South Asia, and since then the country has played a pivotal role in regional politics.

    Historical background
  • Pakistan’s early years were marked by efforts to build institutions and balance civil and military power.
  • The country has experienced alternating periods of democracy and military rule, shaping its political identity.
  • It became a nuclear power in 1998, reinforcing its strategic importance in South Asia.
    Culture and society
  • Pakistan is ethnically diverse, with Punjabis, Sindhis, Pashtuns, Baloch, and others contributing to its rich cultural fabric.
  • Urdu is the national language, while English is widely used in government and education.
  • Islam is the dominant religion, influencing social norms and traditions.
  • The country is known for its vibrant music, poetry, handicrafts, and cuisine — from qawwali and ghazals to biryani and kebabs.
    Current situation (2026)
  • Politically, Pakistan continues to navigate challenges of governance, economic reforms, and balancing civil‑military relations. Diplomatic engagements with neighboring countries remain crucial.
  • Economically, the nation faces inflationary pressures and development hurdles, but also opportunities in technology, trade, and infrastructure projects.
  • Socially, Pakistan grapples with issues of education, healthcare, and climate resilience, while its youthful population offers potential for innovation and growth.

In summary
Pakistan today is a country of contrasts: deeply rooted in history and tradition, yet striving to modernize and stabilize. Its resilience lies in its people, who continue to adapt and push forward despite political and economic uncertainties.
